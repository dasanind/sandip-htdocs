Tentative Schedule

The schedule below is tentative. We will adjust it according to the interest of the class. Most class lectures will be in two parts. In the first part we will give a broad overview of the topic. In the second part, we will go into more details on some sub-topic. This will happen in one of two ways. Either a student will present a paper on a sub-topic; this will be used towards your paper presentation grade. If there is no student presentation for a specific day, then the instructors will go into depth for a specific sub-topic of their choice.

Suggested Papers

Note: The list below will expand as the course progresses, depending on the interest of the class. The listing of papers roughly follows the topics in the schedule above. In class we will cover only a few of the papers, probably one or two from each topic in the schedule. But the list is more extensive to provide you suggestions for papers to present. Of course, please feel free to talk to us if you want to present papers outside this list. The list is also intended to give you a flavor of the richness of different research directions in formal verification.

Equivalence Checking

  • C. Pixley. "A Theory and Implementation of Sequential Hardware Equivalence". IEEE Transactions on CAD for Integrated Circuits and Systems, volume 11(12), pages 1469-1478, 1992. IEEE Computer Society.
  • A. Kuehlmann, A. Srinivasan, D. P. LaPotin. "Verity - A Formal Verification Program for Custom CMOS Circuits". IBM Journal of Research and Development, volume 39(1-2), pages 149-166. 1995.
  • A. Slobodová. "Formal Verification Methods for Industrial Hardware Design". In L. Pacholski and P. Ruzicka, editors, Proceedings of the 28th International Conference on Current Theory and Practice of Informatics (SOFSEM 2001), Piestany, Slovak Republic, 2001, volume 2234 of LNCS, pages 116-135. Springer.
  • J. Baumgartner, H. Mony, V. Paruthi, R. Kanzelman, G. Janssen. "Scalable Sequential Equivalence Checking across Arbitrary Design Transformations". In Proceedings of the 24th International Conference on Computer Design (ICCD 2006), San Jose, CA, October 2006. IEEE.

Model Checking

  • R. E. Bryant. "Graph-Based Algorithms for Boolean Function Manipulation". IEEE Transaction on Computers, volume 35(8), pages 677-691, 1986. IEEE Computer Society.
  • J. R. Burch, E. M. Clarke, D. E. Long, and K. L. McMillan. "Symbolic Model Checking for Sequential Circuit Verification". IEEE Tranaction on CAD for Integrated Circuits and Systems, volume 13(4), pages 401-424, 1994. IEEE Computer Society.
  • E. M. Clarke, O. Grumberg, S. Jha, Y. Lu, and H. Veith. "Counterexample-fuided Abstraction Refinement for Symbolic Model Checking". Journal of the ACM, volume 50(5), pages 752-794, 2003. ACM.

Theorem Proving

  • M. Kaufmann and J S. Moore. "An Industrial Strength Theorem Prover for a Logic Based on Common Lisp". IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering, volume 23(4), 1997, pages 203-213. IEEE Computer Society.
  • L. Paulson. "Designing a Theorem Prover". In S. Abramsky, D. M. Gabbay, and T. S. E. Maibaum, editors, Handbook of Logic in Computer Science, volume II, pages 415-475, 1992. Oxford University Press.
  • M. J. C. Gordon, J. Reynolds, W. A. Hunt, Jr., and M. Kaufmann. "An Integration of HOL and ACL2". In A. Gupta and P. Manolios, editors, Proceedings of the 6th International Conference on Formal Methods in Computer-Aided Design (FMCAD 2006), San Jose, CA, 2006, pages 153-160. IEEE Computer Society.
  • M. Kaufmann, J S. Moore, S. Ray, and E. Reeber. "Integrating External Deduction Tools with ACL2". Journal of Applied Logic, volume 7(1), March 2009, pages 3-25. Elsevier.

Microprocessor Verification

  • B. Brock and W. A. Hunt, Jr. "The DUAL-EVAL Hardware Description Language and Its Use in the Formal Specification and Verification of the FM9001 Microprocessor". Formal Methods in Systems Design, volume 11(1), pages 71-104, 1997. Springer.
  • J. R. Burch and D. L. Dill. "Automatic Verification of Pipelined Microprocessor Control". In D. Dill, editor, Proceedings of the 6th International Conference on Computer-Aided Verification (CAV 1994), Stanford, CA, June 1994, volume 818 of LNCS, pages 68-80. Springer-Verlag.
  • R. B. Jones, J. W. O'Leary, C. H. Seger, M. Aagaard, and T. Melham. "Practical Formal Verification in Microprocessor Design". IEEE Design & Test of Computers, volume 18(4), pages 16-25, 2001. IEEE Computer Society.
  • J. Sawada and W. A. Hunt, Jr. "Verification of FM9801: An Out-of-order Microprocessor Model with Speculative Execution, Exceptions, and Program Modification Capability". Formal Methods in Systems Design, volume 20(2), pages 187-222, 2002. Springer.
  • S. Beyer, C. Jacobi, D. Kröning, D. Leinenbach, and W. J. Paul. "Putting It All Together --- Formal Verification of the VAMP". International Journal on Software Tools and Technology Transfer, volume 8(4-5), pages 411-430, 2006. Springer.

Papers on the remaining topics will be added as the class progresses.
